MIR Lab

Established in 2019 as a research lab, Machine Intelligence and Resilience Laboratory (MIR Lab) at the Indian Institute of Technology, Gandhinagar aspires to develop novel technologies and methodologies that leverage the power of network science, machine learning, data sciences, non-linear dynamics, and process-based understanding to advance the resilience of infrastructure lifelines, including transportation, water, and wastewater networks, and power distribution systems subject to disparate hazards. Interdisciplinary domains such as the impact of weather and climate extremes on networked infrastructure systems, vulnerability assessment of built and natural systems to disparate hazards, and physics-guided machine learning for hydrometeorological processes are our focal areas.

Since its inception, MIR Lab has been associated with various translational research projects, including network science-based decision support system for post-COVID-19 recovery for the city of Ahmedabad, India, revising State Action Plan on Climate Change for the state of Gujarat, and conducting training programmes for mid-level officers to institutionalize capacities on Climate Change Studies and Action in collaboration with GIZ, GmbH, and Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, Government of India
